History of chocolate

Mexico

History of chocolate starts in Latin America, where cacao trees grown wild probably in what is today southeast Mexico.

Spain, 1502

Somehow cacao beans arrived to Spain around 1502, which began importing chocolate to Europe.

Europe, 1585

Chocolate mania spread throughout Europe in 1585. With the high demand for chocolate came chocolate plantations. Europeans started to create other varieties of hot chocolate with cane sugar, cinnamon and other common spices and flavourings.

England

In short amount of time chocolate houses were perfectly known in London, Amsterdam and other European cities.

US, 1641

Chocolate arrived then to Florida in 1641 and in 1773 became a main import goods and it could be enjoyed by people of all classes

Netherlands, 1828

In 1828, Dutch chemist Coenraad Johannes van Houten discovered a way to make a powdered chocolate, what made it easier to mix with water.

England, 1847

In 1847, British chocolatier J.S. Fry created the first chocolate bar made of sugar, cocoa butter and chocolate liquor. From now chocolate was known not only as a beverage.
